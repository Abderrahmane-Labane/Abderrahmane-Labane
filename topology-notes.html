About This Course
Overview

This course offers a semi-rigorous introduction to the mathematical structures underlying theoretical physics. By semi-rigorous, we mean that all definitions and theorems will be stated with mathematical precision, but not all will be proven in full detail. Instead, the focus will be on developing the mathematical intuition and thinking style necessary for understanding and contributing to modern theoretical physics.


We aim to build everything from scratch — no prior background in advanced mathematics or physics is assumed. The only requirement is curiosity and a willingness to learn. Where we skip formal proofs, we'll compensate with rich and illuminating examples drawn from physics and geometry. The ultimate goal is to equip participants with a rigorous mathematical lens and the foundational tools needed to independently approach advanced topics in mathematical physics.
